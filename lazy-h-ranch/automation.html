Final Project CIT 225 – Database Design and Development: Automate the Lazy H Ranch

Summary

The Lazy H Ranch is a piece of property in a remote area (off-grid) of Lincoln County, Wyoming, about 10 miles due north of the town of Kemmerer. “Ranch” is a bit controversial as the only thing on the sage infested high plains grassland are antelope rabbits, ground squirrels, prairie dogs, coyotes, and an occasional bobcat. It currently has a travel trailer, a greenhouse (currently being use for equipment storage,) a small storage building, a water well, a weather station (5 temperature sensors, an anemometer, a rain gauge, solar panels and storage batteries, and 7 security cameras.) I have remote access to the security cameras and the weather station. This summer I plan to connect the water well to the greenhouse and start growing vegetables. That’s going to require additional solar panels, batteries, and some type of remote option for watering, since I don’t live up there. I also plan to monitor and control the power system remotely with a solar controller and some type of controller to decide which “appliances” to use based on battery levels. With the security system and weather station, I see lots of data I can store and analyze to make decisions.
